4.4 crore cases pending

India's judiciary faces a significant backlog of pending cases. As of the last available data, there were approximately 4.4 crore (44 million) cases pending in various courts across the country. This backlog contributes to delays in the justice system.

Efforts to Address Backlog

The Indian government and the judiciary have been taking various measures to address the backlog of cases. These measures include the establishment of fast-track courts, the use of technology for case management, and initiatives to promote alternative dispute resolution methods.

Longest-Pending Case

India's longest-pending case is the Ayodhya dispute, spanning over seven decades, centered on a contested religious site's ownership.

Judicial Productivity

The disposal rate of cases varies by court. Some courts may clear thousands of cases each year, while others have a slower pace.
